"Learning Design of ICT Competency MOOC in relation to ARCS Motivation Model"

The study is to explore the specific learning designs in ICT Competency MOOC that support the ARCS Motivation Model which consists of attention(A), relevance(R), confidence(C) and satisfaction(S). An interviewed with eight respondents (focus group) that had been made, it is shown that only attention, relevance and confidence is supported in ICT Competency MOOC. Satisfaction and the feedback from the lecturer is the main issue raised. Lecture, tutorial and feedback from lecturer are the main service provided by the university thus student satisfaction acts as a benchmark whether they had received quality in learning or not. This is because, when students are satisfied with the learning process, it able to make the online process and the platform alive. This study is performed in small size thus caution should be taken for the generalization of the findings. The findings will help MOOC instructors to provide and aware the usage and completion of the video thus provide more attractive approach to make students eager to join the online learning platform.
